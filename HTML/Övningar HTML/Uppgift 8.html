Chai Tea: Are There Health Benefits?

Posted by Dan Brennan and Margaret Wack.

Green tea receives a lot of praise for its high antioxidant content, but it isn not the only hot beverage that offers a major boost to physical and mental health. Not only does chai tea provide many of the benefits associated with green tea, it has a bold flavor and enticing aroma that few people can resist.

Nutrition Information

Chai tea is a great source of antioxidants such as catechins and theaflavins. These fight oxidative stress and can play a role in preventing cancer and other conditions.

Depending on how it's prepared, chai tea may also include:

  • Calcium
  • Maganese
  • Potassium
  • Vitamin K

Benefits of Chai Tea

1. Rich in antioxidants

Chai tea is a great source of antioxidants, which work to reduce free radicals in the body and promote cellular health, and can even help prevent degenerative diseases and certain forms of cancer. Like other types of tea made from the camellia sinensis tea plant, black tea is extremely high in antioxidants. Cinnamon bark, cardamom, and cloves are also high in antioxidants.

2. Boosts heart health

Ingredients in chai tea have been shown to help boost heart health. Black tea contains flavonoids, which are a special compound that holds the key to many of black teas important health benefits. Flavonoids can help prevent plaque buildup in the bodys arteries, which reduces stress on the heart. Cinnamon has also been shown to help to treat cardiovascular diseases and reduce harmful cholesterol.

3. Fights off colds

If you are feeling under the weather, a cup of chai can actually help to ease symptoms of the common cold, or even ward off colds entirely. Black tea contains catechins with antiviral properties that may contribute to the prevention of common illnesses such as colds and the flu. Ginger contains antimicrobial properties that can help to fight off illnesses like strep throat. Cinnamon contains unique antibacterial properties that can help to protect you against infection and keep you safe and healthy. On top of all that, cardamom and cloves also contain antibacterial and antimicrobial properties, making chai tea a great choice if you are looking to protect your health!
